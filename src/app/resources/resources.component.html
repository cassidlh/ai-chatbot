Background and Significance

We understand that grief is a deeply personal and complex emotion. Many people have limited access to grief support, particularly for college students aged 18-24, who may struggle with financial barriers, long wait times for counseling, and a lack of accessible mental health resources tailored to their needs. Cultural stigmas around mental health create barriers. To help mitigate this, LUMINA AI provides accessible, tailored emotional support.

Creation of Lumina AI

Based on the findings below, we designed our chatbot model to focus on emotional adaptability rather than pure conversation simulation. We incorporated a pre-chat assessment to determine the user's grief type and emotional state, allowing the AI to adjust its responses accordingly. Additionally, we decided to implement a resource hub within the chatbot to provide external mental health support for students who may need more than just AI-driven assistance. Follow-up interviews with select survey respondents provided further insights, helping us refine the chatbot's tone, response structure, and feature set. In essence, we evolved our design into a more interactive, emotionally responsive model that adapts to the user's unique grief journey. Our research reinforced the need for a compassionate, scalable, and culturally aware grief support system tailored specifically to college students, especially those from immigrant backgrounds who may face additional barriers in seeking traditional mental health care.

The initial primary target demographic was college students and supervisors at UCI workplaces. The outcome demographic immigrant/first-gen college students. Interviews helped refine chatbot features.
